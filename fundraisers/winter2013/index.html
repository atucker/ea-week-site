Winter 2013 fundraiser

November 30, 2013 • by John Sturm

This holiday season, Harvard Effective Altruism is fundraising for GiveWell’s top-rated charities—which we think represent our best opportunity to do good in the world. Give back effectively this year—give with us!

Our first $5000 of donations will be matched by an anonymous donor. And don’t wait: our fundraiser will end on January 20th.

Why give now:

It’s always a good time to give to effective charities—you can make a surprisingly large impact on someone’s life! Our fundraiser is an especially good opportunity for three reasons:

First, an anonymous donor has agreed to match our first $5000 of donations—so you can give twice as much.

Second, by spreading the word through our Facebook event, Rally page, and Youtube video, you can convince others to donate!

Third, we’re competing with student groups at Princeton and Rutgers to see who can fundraise the most! I know I’m still sore about our one loss this football season…

Our charities:

GiveDirectly distributes cash to very poor individuals in Kenya and Uganda. Strong evidence indicates that cash transfers lead recipients to spend more on their basic needs (such as food) and may allow recipients to make investments with high returns, with no evidence of large increases in spending on items like alcohol or tobacco.

Schistosomiasis Control Initiative (SCI) supports programs that treat people for parasitic worm infections that cause short-term symptoms such as anemia, and may cause longer-term developmental problems. These worms are extremely inexpensive to treat.

Deworm the World Initiative also seeks to treat parasitic worm infections, but focuses more on advocacy and technical assistance to governments providing deworming and less on direct treatment.

Comparative advantages

GiveDirectly has the most direct and robust case for impact. It also has potential to improve the aid community’s understanding of, and interest in, cash transfer programs.

SCI’s activities have the highest estimated cost-effectiveness; it is most likely that they have the largest impact on people’s lives.

DtWI has the highest potential for leverage (doing extra good through indirect means), as it focuses on influencing governments spending rather than covering all costs with its own funds.

For more in-depth information, you can visit GiveWell’s top charities page, or read their in-depth reviews of GiveDirectly, SCI and DtWI.

How to Donate:

Step 1:

Read about our charities and choose how much to give to each one. Which do you think is the most effective, and why?

Step 2:

For donations under $20, you can give right now on our Rally page. Then email harvardea@gmail.com (from the account you submit with Rally) to tell us how to split your donation between our three charities.

For donations over $20, we ask that you donate directly through GiveWell’s website. So that we can match your donation: after entering your personal information, check “Dedicate my donation in honor of someone” and enter “Name: Harvard Effective Altruism”, “Email Address: harvardea@gmail.com”.

Step 3:

Tell your friends! Help us spread the message of effective giving.

You can join and share our Facebook event, like our Rally page, or watch and share our Youtube video.

About Harvard Effective Altruism

We are a campus group dedicated to spreading the ideas of effective altruism—that we should apply evidence and reason to find the most effective ways to improve the world. Read more on our home page.
