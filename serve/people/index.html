Organizers

John Sturm Co-president

John Sturm ‘15 is a resident of Lowell House and the co-president of HEA. He studies physics and math, and also takes particular interest in economics and meta-ethics. John spends his free time puzzling over paradoxes, playing frisbee and jazz, musing at his blog and trying to escape from civilization.

Ales Flidr Co-president

Ales ‘17 is from the Czech Republic with a longstanding interest in history, philosophy, politics and economics. His passion for global distributive justice and development issues was sparked by an introductory course in philosophy. At high school, he pursued it further, both academically and as a volunteer for Fairtrade. Appreciating the interdisciplinary nature and practical implications of effective altruism, he considers making it his academic focus. Outside of school, he enjoys running, cross-country skiing, reading, being with people he likes and contemplating the meaning of life (preferably with Monty Python).

Aaron Tucker Press Chair

Aaron Tucker ‘16 lives in Adams studying Mind, Brain, and Behavior within Computer Science, and will probably take too many miscellaneous classes to get a secondary. He has a particular interest in rationality, psychology, and culture. He really likes a few books, and enjoys conversation, travel, and tea.

Angie Jo Relations Chair

Angie Jo ‘16 is a Canadian-Korean sophomore in Adams House concentrating in Architectural Studies, with a focus on East Asia and a secondary in Mind, Brain Behaviour. She is currently curious about historical and indigenous construction traditions and how they can be melded with contemporary methods, as well as in using cognitive spatial studies to improve experiences in buildings, landscapes, and cities.

Jesse Shulman Social Chair

Jesse Shulman ‘16 lives in Mather House. He grew up asking questions about high impact philanthropy thanks to his brother, Carl Shulman, who has been a researcher for MIRI and 80K, and sister-in-law, Anna Salamon, who works for CFAR, and remains passionate about how people should give. He is concentrating in Social Studies with a focus on politics, philosophy, and economics and interested in high-impact entrepreneurship, writing, and filmmaking.

Nir Eyal Faculty adviser

Nir Eyal is Associate Professor of Global Health and Social Medicine (Medical Ethics) at the Harvard Medical School. He specializes in medical and public health ethics.


Alumni

Ben Kuhn Co-founder, Co-president

Ben Kuhn ‘14 was a student in Eliot House and co-president of HEA. He studies pure math, but is a closet computer scientist as well. He’s especially interested in rational decision-making, charity research and altruism advocacy and outreach. In addition to altruism and math he enjoys hiking, singing, playing piano, writing at benkuhn.net, contra dancing, and various other strange activities.

Arturo Elizondo Fall 2013 Fellow

Arturo Elizondo ‘14 lived in Lowell House, and was a Government concentrator with a secondary in Economics. He hails from Laredo, Texas on the US-Mexico border and is particularly interested in international development and sustainability issues as they pertain to our food supply. After taking a course on how humans interact with the natural world, he became particularly interested in animal ethics and effective altruism. He is also a huge fan of TED talks!


Members

Andrew Liu Spring 2014 Fellow

Andrew ‘15 is from Eliot House studying math and computer science. He is currently a research assistant in MIT’s Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory investigating computational models of human cognition. Andrew was previously a data scientist and pedagogical researcher at Udacity, an algorithmic trader at Jump Trading, and a finalist in the Siemens and Intel science competitions for his research on transplant rejection at Stanford Medical School.

Elizabeth Santorella Spring 2014 Fellow

Elizabeth Santorella is a second year graduate student in economics. She enjoys knitting, running, bad puns, and being outside.

Aleksandar Makelov Spring 2014 Fellow

Aleksandar Makelov ‘15 lives in Eliot House who is concentrating in mathematics, but also studies theoretical computer science. His interest in ethics was sparked by reasoning about animal suffering and vegetarianism, and he is curious to know about rationally evaluating more general alternatives for improving the world. In his free time, he enjoys playing guitar, writing/blogging, and cycling.

Nick Phillips Spring 2014 Fellow

Nick Phillips ‘16 is a student in Adams House hailing from nearby Needham, Mass. He is a Social Studies concentrator with a focus field related to class identity—his studies in this area have led to an interest in philanthropy. He has an appetite for adventure (and food), enjoys new experiences (and new foods) and is always looking to meet new people (and try new kinds of food). Nick likes to spend his time outside hiking, biking and running. When the Charles is not frozen, he can be found on the river, rowing with Harvard’s lightweight crew.

Ernesto Fernandez Spring 2014 Fellow

Ernesto is a fourth-year Master of Divinity student with an academic focus in modern Christian social ethics in the United States and professional commitment to philanthropy and volunteerism. He is a first generation Cuban-American and Miami native. Ernesto has worked in a variety of Catholic and secular nonprofits on the fundraising as well as grant-making side. He enjoys helping to bring together philanthropists and worthy causes and is passionate about nurturing a more generous culture as well as more powerful forms of generosity. When not feeding his friends home-cooked meals, Ernesto also enjoys pounding them onto the mats in judo (but never too close together).

Chesley Ekelem Spring 2014 Fellow

Chesley Ekelem ‘16 is a student in Kirkland House studying Biology, French, and Global Health. Truly a California girl at heart, she enjoys spending time at the beach, running, and exploring new cultures. She hopes to pursue a career in medicine, specifically for non-profits and NGOs that are aimed at ending health disparities between developed and under-developed countries. She adores the HEA because it gives her a chance to learn from others who have a similar goal of becoming selfless through philanthropy.

Liesl Ulrich-Verderber Fall 2013 Fellow

Liesl Ulrich-Verderber ‘15 lives in Lowell House concentrating in Architectural Studies with a secondary in Economics. Max L'Etoile Fall 2013 Fellow

Max L’Etoile ‘17 is planning on concentrating in engineering or applied physics. He is fascinated by technologies which positively impact public health and well-being. His interest in effective altruism was sparked early in his childhood by his grandfather who would give Max a cash gift every year for Christmas with the stipulation that a portion be given to charity. He is especially interested in finding methods to determine the efficacy of programs which attempt to reduce poverty and poverty related maladies.

May Yang Fall 2013 Fellow

She became more interested in philanthropy and our roles in global injustice after taking Ethical Reasoning 30 (+1 for the general education program!) and hopes to learn more through HEA.
