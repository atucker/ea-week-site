Organizers

Ales Flidr Co-president

Ales ’17 is a college student from the Czech Republic and a prospective economics concentrator. He has a wide array of academic interests, including philosophy, politics, history and economics. He also likes to run, ponder the far future, watch Monty Python and put things on top of other things.

Angie Jo Co-president

Angie Jo ‘16 is a Canadian-Korean sophomore in Adams House concentrating in Architectural Studies, with a focus on East Asia and a secondary in Mind, Brain Behaviour. She is currently curious about historical and indigenous construction traditions and how they can be melded with contemporary methods, as well as in using cognitive spatial studies to improve experiences in buildings, landscapes, and cities.

Aaron Tucker Co-president

Aaron Tucker ‘16 lives in Adams studying Mind, Brain, and Behavior within Computer Science. He’s interested in machine learning, rationality, and society. He really likes a few books, and likes to travel.

John Sturm Adviser, Co-founder

John Sturm ‘15 is a resident of Lowell House and the co-president of HEA. He studies physics and math, and also takes particular interest in economics and meta-ethics. John spends his free time puzzling over paradoxes, playing frisbee and jazz, musing at his blog and trying to escape from civilization.

Aleksandar Makelov Publicity Chair

Aleksandar Makelov ‘15 lives in Eliot House who is concentrating in mathematics, but also studies theoretical computer science. His interest in ethics was sparked by reasoning about animal suffering and vegetarianism, and he is curious to know about rationally evaluating more general alternatives for improving the world. In his free time, he enjoys playing guitar, writing/blogging, and cycling.

Melody Guan Journalism Chair

Eliot House resident Melody Guan ‘16 studies Chemistry and Physics with a secondary in Statistics and hails from Toronto, Canada. Amongst EA-related topics, she is particularly interested in ethics and morality and artificial intelligence as it relates to existential risk. She enjoys scientific research, journalistic writing, hiking, piano, science fiction, and teaching younger students.

Lynette Bye PEP Focus Group Leader

Lynette ’16 is a psychology concentrator in Winthrop interested in positive, moral, and decision-making psychology. She is curious about how to most effectively spread happiness and enjoys conversation, yoga, board games, and pondering practical life philosophy.

Aaron Suduiko PEP Focus Group Leader

Aaron Suduiko ‘17 is a member of Lowell House and prospective concentrator in philosophy or psychology, and is pursuing pre-medical requirements. He enjoys all manners of big and small questions about the nature and science of being, across all disciplines. His interests include theater, identity, and arguing about the myriad ways in which video games present unique intellectual and developmental opportunities to players of all ages.

Grigory Khimulya RFC Focus Group Leader

Grigory Khimulya’18 is a college student from Russia. His academic interests include medical applications of life sciences and data analysis. He is also interested in cognitive science dealing with creativity and decision making. He enjoys biological nonfiction, cooking, sailing and amateur astronomy.

Jesse Shulman Social Chair

Jesse Shulman ‘16 lives in Mather House. He grew up asking questions about high impact philanthropy thanks to his brother, Carl Shulman, who has been a researcher for MIRI and 80K, and sister-in-law, Anna Salamon, who works for CFAR, and remains passionate about how people should give. He is concentrating in Social Studies with a focus on politics, philosophy, and economics and interested in high-impact entrepreneurship, writing, and filmmaking.

Max L'Etoile Organizer

Max L’Etoile ‘17 is planning on concentrating in engineering or applied physics. He is fascinated by technologies which positively impact public health and well-being. His interest in effective altruism was sparked early in his childhood by his grandfather who would give Max a cash gift every year for Christmas with the stipulation that a portion be given to charity. He is especially interested in finding methods to determine the efficacy of programs which attempt to reduce poverty and poverty related maladies.

Current Fellows

Priten Shah Fall 2014 Fellow

Priten H. Shah is a New York resident, who attends Harvard University as a member of the Class of 2018. He is the CEO of Pain, Passion, and Pursuit Inc., a socially conscious company which owns U4SC and works on various projects. Through P3, he started United 4 Social Change to provide a medium for writers to express their views on various topics of importance and to help spur social change using journalism. He is also the Founder and Executive Director of the Teach to Learn Foundation, which aims to increase educational opportunities for the underprivileged through the use of technology. Priten considers education the most fundamental human right and loves to learn new things himself! Never afraid to try something new and explore new domains, he has worked on various projects all somehow related to passion or social change.

Montita Sowapark Fall 2014 Fellow

Montita Sowapark, ‘18, is passionate about regenerative medicine, spoken word poetry, global health policy, and Chipotle. Born in Thailand and raised in Florida, she started donning her boots and leggings in mid-September. Her goal in life, or at least for right now, is to create more value than she captures.

Victor Wu Fall 2014 Fellow

Victor Wu ’16 lives in Cabot House and is interested primarily in moral and political philosophy, while dabbling a little in coding on the side. He’s also slightly obsessed with cats. As a Social Studies concentrator, Victor is thankfully able to take whatever classes are appealing to him in history, government, economics, and normative philosophy and have them all count for his concentration. He became interested in HEA through summer reading in ethics and moral psychology, and he is looking forward to great conversations with this group of interesting and enthusiastic people.

Jose Amich Fall 2014 Fellow

Jose Amich ‘18 is from Barcelona, and plans to concentrate in Molecular and Cellular Biology. Outside classes, he enjoys running, sailing and architecture. He has worked in several laboratories and volunteered teaching English to adults for several years. He sees the HCEA Fellowship as an opportunity to learn about global injustice and how to fight it.

Yifan Wu Fall 2014 Fellow

Yifan Wu ’14 is a student in Eliot House. She is a liberal arts educated software engineer, big data believer, and avid traveler. She has lived in China, the UK and US. Yifan loves good conversations, books, and adventures.

Jeffrey Yan Fall 2014 Fellow

Jeffrey ‘17 from Pfoho is interested in math, computer science, and philosophy. This summer, he worked on Google’s self driving car on the mapping and infrastructure team, which got him excited about working on crazy ideas that seem impossible. He became interested in effective altruism after attending SPARC, an awesome summer camp teaching applied rationality to high school students.

Bilguun Ulammandakh Fall 2014 Fellow

Bilguun Ulammandakh ‘15 is a student in Currier House hailing from Mongolia and Slovakia. He enjoys learning about almost anything, but chiefly studies Philosophy and Neuroscience, with a particular interest in the intersection between the two fields as related to questions on human nature, notion of self, and consequentialist ethics. Bilguun is an avid traveler and in his free time enjoys hiking, cycling, scouring tech blogs and practicing mindfulness meditation.

Ivy So Fall 2014 Fellow

Ivy is in her second and final year of her MBA studies, and has recently spent her summer at the Acumen, a global venture fund tackling poverty. Prior to HBS, Ivy worked to build the market for Social Impact Bonds in Canada. Elizabeth Santorella Spring 2014 Fellow

Chesley Ekelem Spring 2014 Fellow

She adores the HEA because it gives her a chance to learn from others who have a similar goal of becoming selfless through philanthropy.

Carlos Snaider Fall 2013 Fellow

Liesl Ulrich-Verderber Fall 2013 Fellow
